# It’s Today Media – Company Profile  
It’s Today Media is a small, privately-held affiliate and performance marketing firm (headquartered in Incline Village, NV) specializing in digital customer acquisition.  The company “partners with the world’s largest publishers to deliver innovative strategies for growing and monetizing your user base”.  In practice, they **buy media at scale to build email and SMS lists**, emphasizing ROI in all campaigns.  Their services include disciplined media buying, conversion-rate optimization (CRO), full-funnel analytics and “funnel development” (landing pages) to help high‑value publishers scale their audiences.  In short, It’s Today Media “specializes in performance digital advertising” and prides itself on leveraging AI tools to stay on “the cutting edge of marketing execution”.  

- **Industry & Scale:** Listed industries are “Advertising Services/Networks” and “Business Services”.  ZoomInfo and LinkedIn both indicate a very small team: roughly **1–10 employees** (despite LinkedIn’s 11–50 estimate).  Their technology stack (WooCommerce, WordPress, PHP, Cloudflare) reflects a lightweight web infrastructure.  They also appear to operate the financial newsletter site *Market Monitors* (noting the footer on mktmonitors.com cites “Market Monitors (It’s Today Media LLC)”).  
- **Leadership:** Co-founders **George Mecham** (CEO) and **Valerie Dowdle** (COO) built the business (both came from Money Map Press/A‑Mark etc).  Other key executives include **CFO Victoria Orgill** (formerly of finance roles) and **CTO John Mecham**.  (A ZoomInfo list confirms “Victoria Lobosco – Chief Financial Officer” and “John Mecham – Chief Technology Officer”.)  The team also includes an affiliate manager and other marketers, suggesting a lean, multi‑hatted group.  
- **Location & Website:** The firm is based in Incline Village, NV (with a business address also in Baltimore, MD).  Official website is **itstoday.org** (“It’s Today Media – Performance Digital Advertising”).  In 2026 they launched an auxiliary site on **itstoday.media** for recruiting.  Their social presence includes a LinkedIn page (800+ followers) and job listings.

## The $5,000 Build Challenge (Hiring Contest)  
In mid-2026 It’s Today Media opened a **public build contest** to hire an “AI-first” engineer.  The role title is **“Marketing Development Engineer”**, and the prize is **$5,000 plus a full-time job offer**.  The contest brief emphasizes that candidates must *build a real tool* for the company’s media-buying team, using AI and automation to deliver business value.  The company explicitly says it has already “built some cool stuff with AI” but needs someone full-time to expand these tools.  Key points about the contest and role:  

- **Contest Goal:** Create a functional AI‑powered marketing tool that solves a *real problem* in It’s Today Media’s affiliate marketing operations.  Examples suggested on the site include *end-to-end video creative generators, automated ad upload workflows (via their MCP server), landing page generators/CMS, performance dashboards*, etc.  In short, anything that adds value to their paid acquisition funnels is fair game (creative toolchains, ad-platform connectors, workflow automations, analytics dashboards, etc. are all mentioned).  
- **Submission Criteria:** Entries are judged on **business impact and functionality**.  Specifically: (1) Pick a genuine problem for the marketing team and solve it; (2) Be able to explain/convince the judges that it’s valuable; (3) Deliver a working solution (even if “ugly and functional”).  The site stresses *function over form*: a working tool beats a broken polished demo.  **What to submit:** a demo or live URL (preferred), the code repo (GitHub), and a README answering three key questions (what the tool does, why this problem was chosen, and next steps).  AI tools and libraries are explicitly encouraged.  

- **Timeline & Process:** 
  - **Register by July 4, 2026 (11:59 PM ET).**  Interested applicants first complete a 2-minute registration form with their bio and project idea.  Approved applicants receive a private submission link via email.  
  - **Build & Submit by July 4, 2026.**  Final entries (demo + code + README) are due by the deadline.  The company notes that “submissions close July 4, 2026 at 11:59 PM ET”.  
  - **Finalists & Interviews:** Finalists will be notified by ~July 10, 2026.  Finalist interviews follow, and a winner (with job offer) is announced shortly after.  
- **Eligibility & Rules:** Candidates must be ready to take a full-time role.  Requirements include: 40+ hours/week availability, overlap with East Coast business hours, and US work authorization (US citizens, green card holders, or work-authorized residents).  Only one solo submission per person is allowed; group entries are not permitted.  AI/automation tools are encouraged (the company “cares what you build, not how”).  Applicants retain IP on their code unless they accept an offer.  

- **Role Fit:** The contest is explicitly targeted at someone **passionate about marketing and AI**, who can work independently and drive business results.  The company warns it’s *not* a good fit for those who need hand-holding, avoid risk, or dislike data/metrics.  Success in this role means quickly learning the affiliate-marketing workflow and iterating on tools that improve lead generation ROI.  

## Research Findings & Recommendations  
Overall, It’s Today Media appears to be a **growth-stage affiliate marketing operator** (in the financial/publishing vertical) that is now aggressively recruiting AI-skilled talent through this contest.  Key takeaways: 

- **Business Focus:** The company’s core business is lead generation via paid media (Google, Meta, TikTok, Taboola, etc.) and funnel management (landing pages, email lists).  Candidates should familiarize themselves with performance marketing metrics (CPA, CTR, CVR, ROAS, LTV) and AI tools for creative/optimization.  Tools that **directly tie to improving ROI or scale of their campaigns** will resonate most.  
- **Contest Strategy:** If entering, pick a problem in the media-buying/lead gen process. For example, automating ad creative creation, audience research, landing-page personalization, or analytics reporting – all are mentioned as areas of interest.  Be sure to **explain clearly why the problem matters** and demonstrate impact on revenue or efficiency (the judges value real-world relevance).  
- **Submission Tips:** Provide a **live demo or walkthrough** wherever possible, and include a clean code repository.  In the README, answer the three questions succinctly (what it does, why chosen, next features) as instructed.  Show your thought process: the contest explicitly looks at *why you built it* and *what’s next*.  Emphasize any use of AI/agentic systems, as the company is keen on “AI-first engineering”.  
- **Work Culture Fit:** The ideal hire is described as a “doer” who thrives under pressure and learns on the fly.  They value hustle, ownership, and comfort with feedback.  In communications or the README, candidates might highlight prior examples (even small side projects) where they took initiative, used AI tools (e.g. ChatGPT, Claude) to solve marketing problems, and iterated quickly.
